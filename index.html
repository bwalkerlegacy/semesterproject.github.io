Theodore Roosevelt

26th President of the United States

Biography

Theodore Roosevelt became the 26th and youngest President in the nations history on September 14th 1901. He was thrust into the position after the 25th President William McKinley was assassinated by Leon Czlogosz. Theodore Roosevelt was an American politician, statesman, conservationist, naturalist, and writer. He served two years as the governor of New York then later served as the vice president under William McKinley for six months, then assuming the presidency after McKinley's assassination. He served as president of the United States from 1901 to 1909.
